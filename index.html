        What does this mean?
        

            Your browser fingerprint is a collection of device, browser, and behavior attributes that can uniquely identify you among many users.
            Even without cookies, websites can often recognize you using this method.
        

        

            The privacy score above is a simple estimation based on how unique some of your characteristics are (e.g. screen size, language, device memory, CPU cores).
            The more unique, the easier it is to identify you.
        

        

            This demo uses the FingerprintJS library, an open-source tool that estimates browser uniqueness.
            You can view their developer documentation here.

        How to interpret the scores
        
    
            
  • 1 = Unique: This characteristic is uncommon and contributes to making your fingerprint more identifiable.
    • 
            
  • 0 = Common: This characteristic is shared by many users and doesn‚Äôt make your fingerprint stand out.
    • 
        

        

            For example:
        

        
    
            
  • Platform = 1: Your operating system or device type is uncommon (e.g., Linux or unusual OS/browser combos).
    • 
            
  • User Agent = 0: Your browser version and engine are commonly used.
    • 
            
  • Multiple Languages = 0: Single or common language settings (like only English) are typical.
    • 
            
  • Uncommon Screen Resolution = 0: Your screen size and pixel ratio are very common.
    • 
            
  • Timezone Info = 1: Your time zone may be rare or mismatched with your system locale.
    • 
            
  • Low Device Memory = 0: You have a standard or higher amount of memory (e.g., 8 GB or more).
    • 
            
  • Few CPU Cores = 0: Your device has more than 2 cores, which is common today.
    • 
        

        

            Scores are determined based on how rare a given feature is among users. The more rare characteristics you have (i.e., more 1s), the easier it is to uniquely identify your browser/device.
        

        

            For more info, visit the FingerprintJS documentation.
